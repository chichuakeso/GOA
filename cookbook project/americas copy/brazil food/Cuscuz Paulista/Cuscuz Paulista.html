Cuscuz Paulista is a traditional Brazilian dish made from cornmeal, vegetables, and seafood or chicken. Unlike North African couscous, this dish is molded into a cake-like shape and served as a flavorful side or main dish. It originates from São Paulo and is popular at family gatherings and celebrations.

How to Make Vegan Cuscuz Paulista

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cornmeal (fine or medium-ground)
  • 2 1/2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels
  • 1/2 cup chopped hearts of palm
  • 1/4 cup chopped olives
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric (for color and flavor)
  • Boiled chickpeas or tofu slices (for garnish)
  • Tomato slices (for garnish)

Instructions:

1. Sauté the Vegetables:

Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic until soft. Add chopped tomatoes, tomato paste, paprika, turmeric, salt, and black pepper. Cook for 2-3 minutes.

2. Add the Ingredients:

Stir in peas, corn, olives, and hearts of palm. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a simmer.

3. Add the Cornmeal:

Slowly add the cornmeal while stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Cook until the mixture thickens and pulls away from the pan.

4. Assemble the Dish:

Grease a mold or bowl with oil. Arrange sliced tomatoes and boiled chickpeas or tofu at the bottom. Pour the cooked mixture into the mold and press down firmly.

5. Cool and Serve:

Let the cuscuz cool for about 15 minutes, then carefully invert onto a plate. Serve warm or at room temperature.
