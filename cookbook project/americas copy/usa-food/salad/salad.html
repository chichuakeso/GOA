Quinoa, an ancient seed cultivated in South America for thousands of years, is a highly nutritious ingredient packed with protein, fiber, and essential amino acids. It has become a staple in modern healthy eating due to its light, nutty flavor and versatility. One of the most delightful variations is Quinoa & Cranberry Salad, which combines the wholesome goodness of quinoa with the tart sweetness of dried cranberries. This salad is perfect as a light meal, side dish, or even a festive holiday addition. The combination of textures—fluffy quinoa, chewy cranberries, crunchy nuts, and crisp vegetables—creates a refreshing and satisfying dish.

How to Make Quinoa & Cranberry Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped almonds or walnuts
  • 1/2 cup diced cucumber
  • 1/4 red onion (finely chopped)
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp honey or maple syrup
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)

Instructions:

1. Cook the Quinoa:

Rinse the quinoa under cold water to remove bitterness. In a saucepan, bring 2 cups of water or vegetable broth to a boil. Add the quinoa, reduce heat, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes until water is absorbed. Remove from heat and let it cool.

2. Prepare the Ingredients:

While the quinoa cools, chop the cucumber, red onion, and parsley. Toast the nuts in a dry pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until lightly golden.

3. Make the Dressing:

In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, honey (or maple syrup), salt, and black pepper.

4. Assemble the Salad:

In a large bowl, combine the cooled quinoa, cranberries, toasted nuts, chopped vegetables, and crumbled feta (if using). Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.

5. Serve and Enjoy:

Let the salad sit for a few minutes to absorb the flavors. Serve chilled or at room temperature as a nutritious meal or side dish.
