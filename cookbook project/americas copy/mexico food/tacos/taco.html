Tacos date back to ancient Mexico, where indigenous people used corn tortillas to hold fillings like fish, beans, and meats. The word "taco" is believed to have originated from Mexican silver miners in the 18th century, who used small explosive charges wrapped in paper—similar in shape to rolled tortillas. Over time, tacos evolved into a globally loved dish, with countless variations from street-style tacos to Tex-Mex creations.

How to Make Authentic Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 lb (450g) beef, chicken, or mushrooms (for a vegan option)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp chili powder (optional)
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 lime (cut into wedges)
  • 1/2 cup salsa or hot sauce

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Filling:

Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add your choice of beef, chicken, or mushrooms. Season with cumin, paprika, garlic powder, salt, black pepper, and chili powder. Cook until fully done.

2. Warm the Tortillas:

Heat a dry pan over medium heat and warm each tortilla for 30 seconds per side until soft.

3. Assemble the Tacos:

Fill each tortilla with the cooked meat or mushrooms. Top with diced onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.

4. Add Extras:

Serve with salsa, hot sauce, or any other toppings you like.

5. Serve and Enjoy:

Enjoy your homemade tacos while they’re fresh and warm!
