what is hackathon?


A hackathon is an event—usually lasting from a day to a week—where people come together to collaboratively build software or hardware projects. The term “hackathon” is a combination of “hack” (meaning exploratory programming) and “marathon”.

How Does a Hackathon Work?


1.Opening Ceremony • Welcome speech • Rules explanation • Challenge themes introduced (sometimes by sponsors) 2.Team Formation • Participants can bring their own team or form teams at the event. • Ideal team has mixed skills: coding, design, pitching. 3.Idea Brainstorming • Teams choose a problem or theme to tackle. • Decide on a project idea and plan out the solution. 4.Building Phase • Most of the time is spent here: coding, designing, and testing. • Mentors may be available for guidance. • Some teams also focus on business models or user interfaces. 5.Checkpoint(s) (Optional) • Midway updates, feedback from mentors, progress check-ins. 6.Submission • Projects must be submitted before the deadline (usually through a platform like Devpost or GitHub). 7.Presentation/Demo • Teams present their product to judges. • Usually involves a short pitch and a live or recorded demo. 8.Judging & Awards • Criteria often include innovation, usability, technical difficulty, and impact. • Winners receive prizes or opportunities (e.g., funding, internships).

Why Join a Hackathon?


• Learn new technologies and tools
• Meet and collaborate with others
• Turn creative ideas into real prototypes
• Win prizes or get noticed by recruiters
• Add to your resume and portfolio
